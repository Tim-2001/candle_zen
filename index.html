Frequently Asked Questions

Organic candles are typically made from natural ingredients such as soy wax or beeswax, which burn cleaner and emit fewer toxins compared to traditional paraffin candles. They often contain essential oils for fragrance, which can provide therapeutic benefits.

Organic candles are generally considered safer than traditional candles because they do not contain synthetic chemicals or paraffin, which can release harmful substances when burned. They are usually made from natural materials that burn cleaner and produce less soot.

Organic candles typically use natural ingredients such as soy wax, beeswax, or coconut wax. They may also include essential oils for fragrance and natural cotton or hemp wicks. These ingredients are chosen for their sustainability and environmental benefits.

Organic candles can have a positive impact on the environment compared to traditional candles. They often use sustainable ingredients, produce less soot and toxins when burned, and may be packaged in eco-friendly materials. However, it's essential to consider the entire lifecycle of the candle, including production, use, and disposal.

The burn time of organic candles can vary depending on the type of wax and size of the candle. Typically, they burn longer than traditional paraffin candles because they have a lower melting point and may contain denser wax materials like beeswax. Factors such as wick type and candle care also influence burn time.
